On the Subject of Singularity Button

Oh there is one of this. This should be easy. Wait is there more of itself?

This module consists of a singular round button with a screen at the center. The screen may or may not show a character at a certain time interval. To disarm this module, you must disarm ALL instances of Singularity Button attached to the given casing by interacting with them in a specific way. An incorrect combination of actions on the buttons will cause a strike. The display on Singularity Button may or may not change after a strike. The color around the button's display will not change, however.

Appendix: The Disarm Button

It's there for a reason. And yes, you may not like it at all.

On the back of the module is a red rectangular button that says "DISARM MODULE MANUALLY." When pressed, this will automatically disarm the given Singularity Button only when Singularity Button's are put in a "solve state". Due to strange natures on the "solve detection system" for modules that handle synchronized solves like Singularity Button, all Singularity Button's will flip to the back due to the fact that there is no way Singularity Button could directly connect to a module that relies on solves without an override. Notable actions that forbid synchronized solves are, reading a stage on major modules that require an input at the end, and inputting an answer on a module before another solve. No time, solve or strike dependent actions are required for the disarm button to be pressed.

Originally, Singularity Button was going to detect if certain modules were present on the bomb or if there was 1 instance of Singularity Button. In summary, the effort to set up a system for solve dependent detection system was not worth the time at the end.
